What is a ghost font?

A ghost font renders a message as a field of random dots. Dots inside the letter shapes drift in one direction while the background dots drift in another. Human vision segments the two motions instantly, so the text pops out during playback — but pause the video and the letters vanish, because no individual frame contains any difference between text and background. Screenshots, frame extraction, and OCR all come back empty. It is being used as an anti-AI watermark: content agents cannot read, summarize, or act on.

How the decoder breaks it

The trick has one weakness: the message is the motion. The decoder reads the motion directly instead of the frames.

STEP 1

Dense optical flow

Computes a motion vector for every pixel between consecutive frames (OpenCV DIS or Farnebäck).

STEP 2

Background subtraction

The median flow is the background motion. Pixels moving against it score as candidate text.

STEP 3

Drift registration

Phase correlation aligns each score map, so slowly drifting glyphs stack sharply instead of smearing.

STEP 4

Accumulate and read

Scores sum over hundreds of frame pairs, noise cancels, letters remain — then OCR reads them out.
